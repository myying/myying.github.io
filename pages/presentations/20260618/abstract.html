I attended the 21st International EnKF Workshop at Rosendal Fjordhotel, Norway, and gave a talk titled "New features in NEDAS v1.2.0 and applications in geophysical data assimilation".

I showed several applications of NEDAS, our light-weight Python ensemble data assimilation software, to real geophysical DA problems. In the TOPAZ ocean/sea-ice forecasting system, I demonstrated that a new two-scale ensemble perturbation scheme better represents the sea-ice velocity and drift error/spread across scales than the current operational scheme, giving a better fit to observations. I also tested direct assimilation of AMSR2 brightness temperatures using a simplified surface radiative transfer model as the observation operator, as an alternative to assimilating sea-ice concentration retrievals. In the neXtSIM sea-ice model, I showed an alignment-based Updator that corrects linear kinematic features (leads and ridges) using an "optical flow"-style multiscale alignment approach. Finally, I introduced COMEDI, a new EU Horizon project coordinated by NERSC with 11 partners starting in September 2026, which will build on NEDAS to improve interoperability of DA systems for operational ocean and sea-ice forecasting in the Copernicus Marine Service.
